🍳 How to Use the Oven
1
Turn the left knob (Function) to select the cooking mode you need (see modes below).
2
Turn the right knob (Temperature) to set the desired temperature. The display will show the target temp.
3
Wait for preheat. The thermostat light turns off when the oven reaches the set temperature — that's when you put your food in.
4
Place your food on the middle shelf (rack position 2 or 3) unless otherwise needed.
5
When done, turn both knobs back to the off position (●). The cooling fan may continue to run for a few minutes — this is normal.
🔥 Cooking Modes
♨️
Most versatile
Fan Cooking (Convection)
Hot air circulated by fan. Even cooking on any shelf. Best for most dishes — pizza, casseroles, pastries, roasts. Use 20°C lower than traditional recipes.
🔲
Conventional (Top + Bottom Heat)
Classic baking — heat from top and bottom elements. Ideal for cakes, bread, and single-tray baking.
🫕
Grill
Top element only — intense heat from above. For toasting, browning, grilling meat or cheese on top. Keep door closed.
🍕
Bottom Heat
Heat from bottom only. Great for getting a crispy base — pizza, pies, quiches.
💡
Light Only
Turns on the oven light without heating. Useful to check on food.

💡 Important Tips
⚠️ Always preheat
Wait for the thermostat indicator light to turn off before placing food inside. This usually takes 10–15 minutes.
🔥 Hot surfaces!
The oven door, handle, and surrounding surfaces get very hot. Always use oven mitts (provided in the kitchen drawer).
🌀 Cooling fan
You'll hear the cooling fan running during and after use — warm air comes out above the door. This is completely normal and protects the cabinets.
🧹 After use
Please wipe any spills once the oven has cooled. Don't use aluminium foil on the oven floor — it can damage the enamel.
🚫 Please don't
Don't leave the oven unattended when using the grill function. Don't place plastic, paper, or wooden items inside the oven.
💨 Range Hood (Davoline)
The Davoline range hood above the oven has 2–3 speed settings and a light button. Turn it on while cooking to clear steam and odours. Remember to turn it off when you're done.
🔧 Quick Troubleshooting
Oven won't heat up?
Make sure both knobs are turned — one for function, one for temperature. Check that the clock shows the correct time (a flashing '12:00' after a power cut blocks the oven). Set the time using the +/− buttons.
Display flashing 12:00?
There was a power cut. Use the clock buttons on the panel to set the current time. The oven won't operate until the clock is set.
Fan keeps running after turning off?
This is normal. The cooling fan runs until the oven cools down sufficiently. It will stop on its own.
